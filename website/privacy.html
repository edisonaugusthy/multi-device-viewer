Privacy, without the fine-print fog.

The Mobile View & Responsive Tester extension is built to work locally. It has no accounts, advertising, extension telemetry, or remote application backend. The public marketing website uses Amplitude Analytics and Session Replay as described below.

The short version

Your page URLs, responsive workspace, captures, recordings, annotations, saved sets, design references, and generated fix prompts stay on your device. Nothing is automatically uploaded to us.

What stays local

Workspace settings and preferences are stored through Chrome's local extension storage. Screenshots, recordings, and prompts are created only after an action you initiate. Data leaves the extension only when you explicitly copy, download, open, or share it.

Chrome permissions

Tab and scripting access let the extension open the page you selected inside the responsive workspace. Storage preserves local preferences and saved sets. Download and capture capabilities are used only for user-requested exports, screenshots, or recordings.

Websites inside previews

Pages loaded inside the responsive workspace can make their normal network requests. Those requests go to the websites and services the page already uses and remain governed by their own privacy policies.

Website analytics and Session Replay

The public marketing website uses Amplitude to measure visits and product-interest interactions, including installation, source-code, documentation, and FAQ activity. Amplitude Session Replay is enabled for website sessions. This analytics integration is website-only and is not included in the Chrome extension or the pages you test inside it.

Other third-party services

The extension contains no analytics, advertising, or hosted AI SDK. The fix-prompt feature formats text locally and copies it only when you press the copy button.

Open-source verification

You do not have to take these statements on trust. Review the extension's implementation, permissions, and storage behavior in the public source repository.

Contact

Questions, privacy reports, and corrections can be filed in the public issue tracker.
